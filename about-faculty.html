Dr. Goteiner was recently evaluated as a Clinical Professor of Clinical Professor for the period July 1, 2002, to June 30, 2003. Exerpts of that evaluation are as follows:

Teaching
Dr. Goteiner's academic appointment consists of one day per week. This past year his teaching assignment consisted of directorship of the Postgraduate Current Literature Review (2 1/2 hours every other week), directorship of the Surgical Case Conference (1 hour per month) and one day per week equally divided amongst undergraduate and postgraduate clinical teaching. He received an exemplary teaching evaluation, noting his significant contributions to department programs.

Research & Scholarship
Dr. Goteiner funded research project, "The relationship between professional dental care, endotoxin levels, interleukin profile and myocardial infarction" is in its final stages. It is anticipated that the data will be completely analyzed within the next few months. A preliminary evaluation of the data indicated that the project was well chosen and executed.

Service
Dr. Goteiner's service has included extramural presentations and consultantship at Morristown Memorial Hospital. His goal relative to Service -- a continuing education course presentation -- was fulfilled.

Overall Evaluation and Comments
Dr. Goteiner has continued to make a significant contribution to the department. He has devoted considerable effort to ensuring maximum benefit to students through the Current Literature Review course. Additionally, his clinical teaching is of the highest standard.

Long-Term Goals
To emphasize a conceptual review of the literature where students grasp a broad knowledge of concepts rather than isolated facts. Goals and objectives for next evaluation period: to bring the pilot research project to completion and then pursue further funding, to present the results of the research project at a national scientific meeting, and to develop a program of video-conferencing.
