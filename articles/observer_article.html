Kids Who Care Get A Reward

Chester dentist David Goteiner and his wife, Carrie, have been honoring students in the area since 1985.

Bond and acclaim granted by Chester dentist

By CAMILLA SOMERS, Contributing Writer
Published: Jul 31st, 7:12 AM
WASHINGTON TWP. – William Reynolds of Long Valley and Ryan Shirtz of Mendham are teenagers who care and a Chester dentist wants the community to know about them.

The Chester dentist, David Goteiner,  said Reynolds and Shirtz are among a group of young people who “make America America.”

That is why Goteiner and his wife, Carrie, have been giving community service awards to area teenagers since 1985. The Goteiners have annually provided $200 Savings Bonds to recognize students’ volunteer work from West Morris Central, Mendham,  Roxbury, Mount Olive, Hackettstown and Randolph high schools.

Reynolds, a student at West Morris Central High School, and Shirtz, a student at Mendham High School, are among this year’s six recipients.

“The award is much more than money,” Goteiner said. “It’s really about recognition.”

The dentist said that each year, he sends a notice of the award to high school officials, who then organize a committee and nominate a deserving student.

The award criteria are simple: The winner should be someone who does not seek recognition, who demonstrates high standards of honesty and responsibility, and exemplifies “the citizen who cares for and contributes to his or her community through action.”

After they are chosen, the winners, who are not informed of their nomination, are recognized at their high school’s end-of-the-year awards ceremony with a certificate and the bond.

Goteiner said he wanted to help dispel the stereotype of teenagers as indifferent or uninvolved.

“What we’re trying to do is shed a little light on teenagers who really care,” he said. 

Reynolds, 18, is a cadet-level emergency medical technician in Long Valley and has responded to more than 200 calls, a feat that earned him “Rookie of the Year” from his first aid squad.

He also completed his Eagle Scout project last October, recording the location of veterans’ graves in a local cemetery. The findings were later presented to the American Legion Post, which will file the information with area libraries, according to

Lori Gleason, Reynolds’ guidance counselor at West Morris Central High School, who submitted a letter nominating Reynolds for the award.

In September, Reynolds will be a student at Worcester (Mass.) State College where he plans to major in nursing and concentrate in emergency medicine.

Shirtz, also 18, was one of three presidents of Mendham High School’s Service Club. The  club advisor, Jennifer Brown, said Shirtz has been extremely involved in and “a vital part” of the group.

Shirtz said he helped with a canned food and Christmas toy drive the club organizes each year. He said the 2008 canned food drive alone kept the Chester Food Pantry stocked for six months.

Shirtz said he plans to attend John Hopkins University in Baltimore, Md., in the fall.

Asked about the significance of volunteerism, Shirtz said, “It’s important to give back to anyone that you can if you have the ability. It’s really a responsibility.”
